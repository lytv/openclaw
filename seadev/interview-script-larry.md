# Interview Script: Project Coordinator - ShedPro/SeaDev
## Role: Larry - Hiring Manager & Interviewer

### Interview Framework Total: 60 minutes

Phase 1 - Opening & Ice Breaker (5 min)
Phase 2 - Background & Motivation (10 min)
Phase 3 - Technical Skills & Project Management (20 min)
Phase 4 - Client Management & Communication (15 min)
Phase 5 - Cultural Fit & Values (5 min)
Phase 6 - Candidate Questions (5 min)

### Phase 1: Opening & Ice Breaker
**Goal**: Establish rapport, explain the interview structure, create comfortable environment
- [ ] Welcome candidate, introduce myself as Senior Interviewer at SeaDev
- [ ] Explain we'll discuss their background, skills, and the Project Coordinator role at ShedPro
- [ ] Confirm they're ready to discuss their experience in both English and Vietnamese
- [ ] Quick ice breaker: "What's one thing from your background that would surprise me?"

### Phase 2: Background & Motivation (10 min)
**Goal**: Understand career progression and motivation for this specific role

**Q1**: "Walk me through your resume focusing on your project coordination/account management experience."
*Follow-up questions:*
- What specifically attracted you to client-facing roles?
- How did your previous experiences prepare you for this type of intersection between operations and client success?

**Q2**: "What do you know about SeaDev and ShedPro, and why does this opportunity interest you?"
*Follow-up questions:*
- Our mission involves both profit and spiritual impact - how does this resonate with your personal values?
- What interests you about serving the North American shed/building industry specifically?

### Phase 3: Technical Skills & Project Management (20 min)
**Goal**: Assess project coordination competencies for software/SaaS environment

**Q3**: "Describe a complex project you coordinated from setup through execution. What was your role, and how did you manage multiple stakeholders?"
*STAR follow-ups:*
- When setting up new client accounts/projects, what systematic approach do you take?
- How do you ensure nothing falls through the cracks during onboarding?
- Share a time when technical issues arose - how did you escalate and communicate with your team and client?

**Q4**: "ShedPro clients are North American shed businesses who may not be technically sophisticated. How would you approach training and setup for non-technical clients?"
*Follow-up:*
- Give me a specific example of how you'd explain a technical concept in simple terms.
- How would you structure a training session for a first-time SaaS user?

**Q5**: "You're working with a US-based Account Manager and Vietnamese development team - describe your communication strategy across different time zones and cultures."
*Follow-up:*
- What tools and methods do you prefer for managing this type of distributed collaboration?
- How do you ensure requirements are clearly understood on both sides?

**Q6**: "Walk me through how you'd monitor and track client account performance. What metrics would you focus on, and how would you identify growth opportunities?"
*Follow-up:*
- How would you present this data to clients who aren't data-savvy?
- Share an example when data/performance insights helped you improve client satisfaction or retention.

### Phase 4: Client Management & Communication (15 min)
**Goal**: Assess relationship building and customer success skills

**Q7**: "Tell me about a time when a client was frustrated or dissatisfied. How did you handle the situation and what was the outcome?"
*STAR method:*
- Situation: Client frustration reason
- Task: Your role in resolution
- Action: Steps you took
- Result: Client relationship outcome

**Q8**: "ShedPro builds relationships with shed manufacturers/dealers across North America. How would you adapt your communication style when working with very different types of businesses - from small family operations to large manufacturers?"

**Q9**: "Our growth goal is to scale client accounts over time. Give me an example where you successfully grew a client relationship from basic setup to expanded services."

**Q10**: "Describe your experience with CRM systems and how you've used them for client relationship management. Any specific tools you're proficient with?"

### Phase 5: Cultural Fit & Values (5 min)
**Goal**: Assess alignment with company culture and remote work culture

**Q11**: "Our team values include caring for team members and community transformation. Share a time when you went above and beyond for a team member or contributed to your community."

**Q12**: "Remote work requires strong self-management. Tell me about your approach to maintaining high productivity and communication in a distributed team."

### Phase 6: Candidate Questions (5 min)
**Goal**: Address candidate questions, explain next steps
- [ ] Invite candidate questions about the role, team, or company
- [ ] Explain growth opportunities and career paths
- [ ] Discuss working relationship with US Account Manager
- [ ] Outline interview process next steps
- [ ] Thank candidate for their time

### Evaluation Criteria Matrix
**Communication Skills (25%)**
- English fluency for US market communication
- Ability to explain technical concepts simply
- Clear, professional client communication

**Project/Account Management (25%)**
- Systematic approach to client setup
- Tracking and reporting capabilities
- Issue escalation and resolution

**Relationship Building (20%)**
- Client relationship management experienceS
- Cultural sensitivity for North American market
- Growth mindset for account expansion

**Technical Adaptability (15%)**
- Quick learner for SaaS platforms
- CRM and project management tool proficiency
- Digital marketing understanding

**Cultural Fit (15%)**
- Alignment with spiritual mission/global impact
- Remote work self-management
- Collaborative team approach

### Special Notes for Interview
- **Language Assessment**: Candidate must demonstrate ability to communicate effectively in English with US clients
- **Industry Context**: Despite being software-focused, they'll manage shed manufacturing clients - assess ability to learn industry-specific requirements
- **Cultural Bridge**: Key success factor is being able to connect Vietnamese development team with US business clients
- **Scalability Mindset**: Look for candidates who think in terms of systematic approaches rather than one-off solutions
