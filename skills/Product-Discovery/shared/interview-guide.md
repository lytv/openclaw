# Product Discovery Interview Guide

Complete question bank for conversational product discovery interviews based on the Product-Market Fit Pyramid.

## Interview Principles

1. **Conversational**: Not a rigid questionnaire - adapt based on responses
2. **Curious**: Ask "why" and "tell me more" often
3. **Nonjudgmental**: No right/wrong answers - just learning
4. **Specific**: Push for concrete examples, not generalizations
5. **Problem-focused**: Understand pain before jumping to solutions

---

## Module A: Target Customer (Who?)

**Goal**: Crystal-clear understanding of WHO has the problem

### Opening Questions

**Q1: Initial Pitch**
> "Tell me about the product idea you're considering."

*Listen for*: Solution-focus vs. problem-focus
*Follow-up*: If solution-heavy, redirect to problem

**Q2: Customer Identification**
> "Who specifically is this for?"

*Red flags*: "Everyone", "All businesses", "Anyone who..."
*Good answer*: Specific role/demographic/behavior
*Follow-up*: "Can you describe a specific person who has this problem?"

### Narrowing Down

**Q3: Persona Details**
> "Tell me more about [customer type]. What's a typical day like for them?"

*Probe areas*:
- Demographics: Age, location, income, education
- Role: Job title, responsibilities, company size
- Psychographics: Values, attitudes, lifestyle
- Behaviors: Tools they use, habits, workflows

**Q4: Problem Context**
> "Walk me through a recent time when [customer] experienced [problem area]."

*Listen for*:
- Specific triggers (When does problem occur?)
- Frequency (How often?)
- Context (Where? With whom?)

**Q5: Current Alternatives**
> "How do they handle this today?"

*Options*:
- Direct competitors (Product A, B, C)
- Workarounds (Excel, manual process)
- Nothing (They just tolerate pain)

### Segmentation

**Q6: Multiple Segments**
> "Are there different types of customers with this problem?"

*If yes*: "Which segment will you focus on first? Why?"
*Framework*: Pick the segment with:
- Highest pain (most eager to solve)
- Easiest to reach (you have access)
- Best economics (willing/able to pay)

```markdown

**Primary Segment**: [Name]

- Values: [What they care about]

- Tools used: [List]

> "[Their words about the problem]"
```

## Module B: Underserved Needs (What Problem?)

**Goal**: Identify HIGH importance, LOW satisfaction needs (opportunity gaps)

### Problem Discovery

**Q7: Pain Point Identification**
> "What frustrates [customer] most about [current situation]?"

*Technique*: "Five Whys" - Keep asking "why is that frustrating?"
*Goal*: Get to root cause, not symptoms

**Q8: Importance Rating**
> "On a scale of 1-10, how important is solving [pain point] to them?"
> "Why did you say [number]?"

*Calibration*:
- 9-10: Top priority, urgent pain
- 7-8: Important but not critical
- 4-6: Nice to have
- 1-3: Don't really care

**Q9: Current Satisfaction**
> "How satisfied are they with how [current solution] addresses this?"
> "On a scale of 1-10?"
> "What specifically makes it [satisfying/unsatisfying]?"

*Calibration*:
- 9-10: Extremely satisfied (hard to displace)
- 7-8: Pretty satisfied (incremental improvement needed)
- 4-6: Neutral (clear room for improvement)
- 1-3: Very unsatisfied (desperate for alternative)

### Opportunity Gap Analysis

**Q10: Gap Identification**
> "Looking at importance vs. satisfaction, where are the biggest gaps?"

*Framework*:
| Pain Point | Importance (1-10) | Satisfaction (1-10) | Gap |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------|-----|
| [Pain 1] | 9 | 3 | **6** ← OPPORTUNITY |
| [Pain 2] | 5 | 4 | 1 ← Skip |
| [Pain 3] | 8 | 8 | 0 ← Red ocean |

*Target*: Importance 8+ AND Satisfaction ≤5

### Jobs-to-be-Done Frame

**Q11: JTBD**
> "When [customer] uses [current solution], what are they really trying to accomplish?"

*Format*: "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]"
*Example*: "When I'm grocery shopping with kids, I want to get in and out fast, so I can minimize tantrums"

**Q12: Success Criteria**
> "How do they know they've successfully solved [problem]?"

*Listen for*: Specific, measurable outcomes

### Capture Template

```markdown
## Underserved Needs

### Importance vs. Satisfaction Matrix

| Need / Pain Point | Importance (1-10) | Current Satisfaction (1-10) | Gap | Opportunity? |
|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------------|-----|--------------|
| [Need 1] | [X] | [Y] | [X-Y] | High/Med/Low |
| [Need 2] | [X] | [Y] | [X-Y] | High/Med/Low |
| [Need 3] | [X] | [Y] | [X-Y] | High/Med/Low |

### Primary Opportunity

**Core Underserved Need**: [The #1 pain with biggest gap]

**Current Workaround**: [How they cope today]

**Pain Quote**:
> "[Customer words describing frustration]"

**Job-to-be-Done**:
When [situation], [customer] wants to [goal], so they can [outcome].

**Success Looks Like**:
- [Measurable outcome 1]
- [Measurable outcome 2]
```

## Module C: Value Proposition (Why You?)

**Goal**: Define differentiated positioning using Kano Model

### Competitive Landscape

**Q13: Alternatives**
> "What other solutions exist for this problem?"

*Categories*:
- Direct competitors (same solution)
- Indirect competitors (different approach, same job)
- Non-consumption (they just deal with it)

**Q14: Competitive Gaps**
> "What do existing solutions do well? What do they miss?"

*Listen for*: Unmet needs that create your wedge

### Kano Model Application

**Q15: Must-Haves (Table Stakes)**
> "What features are absolutely required - if missing, it's a deal-breaker?"

*Examples*:
- Security (for enterprise)
- Mobile access (for on-the-go)
- Basic functionality (search, save, share)

*Strategy*: You MUST have these, but don't compete here

**Q16: Performance Benefits**
> "What ONE attribute will you be significantly better at than alternatives?"

*Dimensions*:
- Speed (faster results)
- Quality (more accurate)
- Ease of use (simpler workflow)
- Price (cheaper)
- Customization (more flexible)

*Rule*: Pick ONE. You can't win on all dimensions.

*Example*: Google chose "Relevance" (not index size or freshness)

**Q17: Delighters (Unexpected Wow)**
> "What will surprise and delight users - something they don't expect?"

*Characteristics*:
- Not expected (so no dissatisfaction if missing)
- Creates emotional response ("Wow!")
- Often the marketing hook

*Examples*:
- Dropbox: "Works like magic" (invisible sync)
- Tesla: Over-the-air updates
- Spotify: Discover Weekly

### Positioning Statement

**Q18: Value Prop Articulation**
> "In one sentence: For [customer], who [need/pain], our solution is [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [alternative], we [unique differentiator]."

*Example*:
"For busy parents, who struggle to plan healthy meals, MealPal is a meal planning app that generates plans in 60 seconds. Unlike meal kit services, we don't require subscriptions or upfront costs."

### Capture Template

```markdown
## Value Proposition

### Competitive Landscape

**Direct Competitors**: [List]
**Indirect Competitors**: [List]
**Non-consumption**: [% who don't use anything]

**Competitive Gaps**:
- ✅ Strengths of existing: [What they do well]
- ⚠️ Weaknesses: [What they miss]

### Kano Analysis

#### Must-Haves (Table Stakes)
- [Feature 1] - Why: [Required because...]
- [Feature 2] - Why: [Required because...]
- [Feature 3] - Why: [Required because...]

*Strategy*: Include all, but don't market these

#### Performance Dimension (Your Winning Attribute)
**Chosen Dimension**: [e.g., Speed, Quality, Ease of Use]

**Competitive Benchmark**:
| Competitor | [Your Dimension] Score |
|------------|------------------------|
| Competitor A | [X] |
| Competitor B | [Y] |
| **YOUR PRODUCT** | **[Z]** ← Significantly better |

*Why this dimension*: [Strategic reasoning]

#### Delighters (Unique Features)
- [Delighter 1] - Why unexpected: [...]
- [Delighter 2] - Why exciting: [...]

*Marketing angle*: [How you'll communicate this]

### Positioning Statement

For [Target Customer],
who [Core Underserved Need],
our product is a [Category]
that [Primary Benefit].

Unlike [Main Alternative],
we [Unique Differentiator].

*One-sentence pitch*:
> "[Elevator pitch]"
```

## Module D: Feature Set (What to Build?)

**Goal**: Scope MVP that delivers value proposition without waste

### Feature Brainstorming

**Q19: Feature Ideas**
> "If you could wave a magic wand, what would this product do?"

*Let them brainstorm*: Capture everything (don't judge yet)

### MVP Scoping

**Q20: Core Value**
> "If you could only build 3 features for your first version, what would they be?"
> "Why those three?"

*Criteria*:
- Delivers on value proposition
- Solves core pain
- Creates "aha moment"

**Q21: Success Metrics**
> "For each feature, how will you know it's successful?"

*Push for*: Specific, measurable outcomes
*Format*: "[X]% of users will [action] within [timeframe]"

**Q22: Out of Scope**
> "What are you explicitly NOT building in V1?"

*Why important*: Prevents scope creep, clarifies focus
*Examples*: "No mobile app", "No integrations", "Single user only"

### ROI Prioritization

**Q23: Impact vs. Effort**
> "For each feature: High/Med/Low impact? High/Med/Low effort?"

*Framework*:
| Feature | Impact | Effort | ROI | Priority |
|---------|--------|--------|-----|----------|
| [F1] | High | Low | 🔥🔥🔥 | P0 (MVP) |
| [F2] | High | High | 🔥 | P1 (Post-MVP) |
| [F3] | Low | Low | 🔥 | P2 (Nice to have) |
| [F4] | Low | High | ❄️ | P3 (Never) |

*MVP rule*: Only High Impact / Low-Med Effort

```markdown

- ❌ [Feature] - Why later: [Reasoning]

- ❌ [Feature] - Why later: [Reasoning]
```

## Interview Flow Tips

### Start Broad, Then Narrow
1. "Tell me about your idea" (Open)
2. "Who is this for?" (Segment)
3. "Tell me about [specific person]" (Persona)
4. "What frustrates them about [specific situation]?" (Pain)

### Use Silence
After asking a question, WAIT. Let them think. Resist urge to fill silence.

### Probe Vague Answers
- "Tell me more about that"
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "Why is that?"
- "What do you mean by [their word]?"

### Redirect Solution-Focus
If they jump to features:
> "I love your excitement about the solution! Before we get there, help me understand the problem better. Tell me about..."

### Capture Quotes
When they say something insightful in their own words, write it down verbatim. Use it in reports.

### End with Reflection
> "Great conversation! Let me summarize what I heard:
> - Target customer: [Summary]
> - Core pain: [Summary]
> - Your unique angle: [Summary]
> - MVP scope: [Summary]
>
> Did I miss anything important?"

## Difficult Scenarios

### Scenario 1: They Don't Know Their Customer
**Signal**: "It's for everyone"

**Response**:
> "Let's start with one segment. Who do you think would feel the most pain from [problem]? Great, let's focus on them first."

### Scenario 2: They're Married to Solution
**Signal**: Keep talking about features, not problems

**Response**:
> "I can see you've thought a lot about the solution. I want to make sure we're solving the right problem. Imagine your product didn't exist - what would [customer] be struggling with?"

### Scenario 3: No Clear Pain Point
**Signal**: Problem importance scores are all 5-6/10

**Response**:
> "Based on what you're telling me, it sounds like this is a 'nice to have' rather than a 'must have.' That's a yellow flag. Let me ask: would [customer] actively seek out a solution, or would you need to convince them they have a problem?"

*Follow-up*: Consider pivoting to a more painful problem

### Scenario 4: Unrealistic Scope
**Signal**: "MVP" has 20 features

**Response**:
> "These are all great ideas! But let's think about what gets you to your first 'aha moment' with a user. If you could only test ONE hypothesis, what would it be? Let's build just enough to test that."

## Interview Checklist

Before ending interview, ensure you captured:

- [ ] Clear target customer (specific persona)
- [ ] At least 3 pain points with Importance ratings
- [ ] At least 3 pain points with Satisfaction ratings
- [ ] Identified opportunity gap (Importance 8+, Satisfaction ≤5)
- [ ] Competitive alternatives listed
- [ ] Must-have features (table stakes)
- [ ] Performance dimension (your winning attribute)
- [ ] At least 1 delighter
- [ ] 3-5 MVP features with success metrics
- [ ] Out-of-scope items explicitly stated
- [ ] At least 2 direct quotes from user's perspective

If any missing → Ask follow-up questions before proceeding to research
