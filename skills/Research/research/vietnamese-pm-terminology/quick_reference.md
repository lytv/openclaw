# Vietnamese PM Terminology - Quick Reference Card

## Core Terms at a Glance

### Most Common Translations

| English | Vietnamese | When to Use |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Product Owner | Chủ sản phẩm | Formal, Scrum contexts |
| Stakeholder | Các bên liên quan | All contexts |
| Backlog | Backlog / Danh sách công việc | Keep "backlog" in tech contexts |
| Sprint | Sprint | Usually keep English |
| Agile | Agile / Linh hoạt | Both acceptable |
| MVP | Sản phẩm khả thi tối thiểu | Or keep "MVP" |
| User Story | Câu chuyện người dùng | Standard translation |
| KPI | KPI / Chỉ số hiệu suất | Usually keep "KPI" |
| Roadmap | Lộ trình | Direct translation |
| Feature | Tính năng | Direct translation |

## Translation Principles

### Keep in English
- Technical acronyms: KPI, MVP, DAU, MAU, CAC, LTV
- Framework names: Agile, Scrum, Sprint
- Role titles: Scrum Master, Product Owner

### Translate to Vietnamese
- Conceptual terms: Stakeholder, Backlog, Roadmap
- Descriptive terms: User Story, Use Case, Pain Point
- Communication terms: Alignment, Buy-in, Feedback

## Sample Sentences

**English:** "The Product Owner manages the product backlog and prioritizes features for each sprint."

**Vietnamese:** "Chủ sản phẩm quản lý backlog sản phẩm và ưu tiên các tính năng cho mỗi sprint."

**English:** "We need stakeholder alignment before the release."

**Vietnamese:** "Chúng ta cần đồng thuận từ các bên liên quan trước khi phát hành."

## Key Insight

Vietnamese PM practice uses **hybrid terminology**:
- English for technical/framework terms
- Vietnamese for conceptual/communication terms
- Consistent within teams and organizations
