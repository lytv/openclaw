# Vietnamese Product Management Terminology Research Report

**Research Date:** January 21, 2025
**Topic:** Best Practices for Translating PM Terminology from English to Vietnamese

## Executive Summary

This research compiles best practices for translating product management terminology from English to Vietnamese. While comprehensive published Vietnamese PM glossaries are limited, the Vietnamese tech community has established conventions through industry practice, translated books, and community knowledge.

## Industry Standards & Best Practices

### Translation Principles

1. **Keep Technical Terms in English**
   - Agile terms (Agile, Scrum, Sprint) often stay in English
   - Standard practice in Vietnamese tech companies

2. **Translate Descriptive Terms**
   - Concepts with clear Vietnamese equivalents (stakeholder, backlog)
   - When meaning would be obscured by English terms

3. **Use Established Conventions**
   - Follow what major tech companies (Google Vietnam, Shopee VN, Grab VN) use
   - Match terminology in Vietnamese product documentation

4. **Consider Context**
   - Internal communications: more English mixed
   - External communications: more Vietnamese preferred
   - Customer-facing: Full Vietnamese translation

### Code-Switching Patterns

Vietnamese PMs commonly use:
- **English**: Technical terms, metrics (KPI, MAU, DAU), Agile framework terms
- **Vietnamese**: Strategy concepts, team communication, customer-facing terms

Example mixed sentence: "Chúng ta cần define rõ product vision và communicate với stakeholders."

## Key Insights for Content Translation

### For Tweets/Essays

1. **Target Audience Matters**
   - Technical audience: More English terms acceptable
   - General audience: Full Vietnamese preferred

2. **Terminology Consistency**
   - Pick one approach and stick with it
   - Don't mix approaches mid-content

3. **Explain English Terms**
   - When using English terms, provide Vietnamese context
   - Example: "Sprint (phiên/phát triển)"

### Recommended Approach

For cyber•Fund content targeting Vietnamese audience:

```
Product Management Terms in Vietnamese:
- PM → Quản lý sản phẩm (PM)
- Stakeholder → Các bên liên quan
- Backlog → Danh sách công việc (backlog)
- Sprint → Sprint (with explanation)
- MVP → Sản phẩm khả thi tối thiểu (MVP)
- User Story → Câu chuyện người dùng
```

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Literal Translations**
   - Avoid translating terms that have no natural Vietnamese equivalent
   - Example: Don't translate "agile" as "nhanh nhẹ linh hoạt" unnecessarily

2. **Inconsistent Terminology**
   - Define terms once and use consistently
   - Create a glossary for long-form content

3. **Over-Translation**
   - Technical acronyms (KPI, MVP, DAU) often stay in English
   - Vietnamese PM community accepts this standard

4. **Cultural Mismatches**
   - Some Western PM concepts don't translate cleanly
   - Adapt explanations to Vietnamese business context

## Recommendations for Content Creation

### Approach 1: Full Vietnamese with English in Parenthesis

```
"Sản phẩm khả thi tối thiểu (MVP) giúp teams..."
```

### Approach 2: English Terms with Vietnamese Explanations

```
"Theo Scrum framework, Sprint là một chu kỳ phát triển..."
```

### Approach 3: Hybrid (Recommended)

For most effective communication with Vietnamese PMs:

- Keep English technical terms that are industry standard
- Translate conceptual and strategic terms
- Use consistent glossary throughout content

---

## Conclusion

Vietnamese PM terminology follows a **hybrid approach**:
- Technical and framework terms often stay in English
- Strategic and conceptual terms translate to Vietnamese
- Industry practice drives standardization more than formal guidelines

For best results, align with what Vietnamese tech companies actually use in practice rather than seeking formal published standards.
